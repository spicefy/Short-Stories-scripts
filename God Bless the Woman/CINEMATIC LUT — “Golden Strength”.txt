📌 Premiere Pro – Lumetri Settings
1️⃣ Basic Correction

Input LUT: None
White Balance:

Temperature: +8

Tint: +4 (magenta)

Tone:

Exposure: +0.2

Contrast: +15

Highlights: -30

Shadows: +20

Whites: -10

Blacks: -8

This keeps skin rich without crushing detail.

2️⃣ Creative Section

Faded Film: 5

Sharpen: 10

Vibrance: +20

Saturation: 105

3️⃣ Curves (Important for Skin Tone)
RGB Curve:

Slight S-curve

Lift shadows slightly

Soft highlight roll-off

Red Channel Curve:

Lift midtones slightly (boosts warmth naturally)

Blue Channel Curve:

Slight lift in shadows (adds cinematic cool depth)

Slight drop in highlights (warmer skin)

4️⃣ Color Wheels & Match
Shadows:

Push slightly toward teal/blue (very subtle)

Midtones:

Push gently toward orange

Highlights:

Push toward warm yellow

Intensity should be subtle — cinematic, not Instagram.

5️⃣ HSL Secondary (Skin Protection — VERY IMPORTANT)

Use eyedropper to select her skin.

Refine Hue + Sat + Luma.

Soften (Blur 10–15).

Then:

Increase Temp slightly (+5)

Increase Saturation slightly (+5)

Increase Exposure slightly (+0.1)

This keeps her skin glowing while background shifts.

Rich golden skin highlights

Cinematic realism
